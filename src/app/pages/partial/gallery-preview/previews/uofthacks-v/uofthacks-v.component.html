This is concept art I created for U of T Hacks VI, as part of a request! U of T Hacks is an annual recurring hackathon held at U of T St. George campus -- a programming competition for students to create something novel in a span of (just over) a day. This particular design was not eventually used for the event. Nevertheless, it was still fun making it!
The U of T Hacks brand has consistently revolved around the Toronto skyline at night, and for my rendition, I decided to continue that tradition.

This design uses various forms of light. There are building lights, sparks, and starlight!

The centre piece of the Toronto skyline is the CN Tower. I wanted to make the tower as realistic as possible, while still being unique.
Skyline buildings took up the majority of the piece. The building lights are on because there are ideas at work! At night is when a lot of the magic happens at hackathons.

Indeed, hackathons are no doubt hectic most times. And so, the atmosphere I was aiming for was, in conjuction to excitement and ideas: calm, peaceful, and warm. Hopefully participants (and sponsors, and volunteers!) do get a chance to take a breather and appreciate how pretty the city can be sometimes! Yes, I am a big fan of Toronto (despite all of it's flaws).

I gave the building lights, as well as the tiered skylines a soft glow as they fade into the distance.
Lastly, the title. To make it just a bit more magical, I added sparks around it, complementing the warm glow of the skyline. Sparks symbolize the creation of ideas. U of T Hacks is where ideas happen!
