A (brief) introduction

I'll describe who I am currently — minus nitty-gritty career and academic aspects because there's plenty of that in the about page.

I'm Fides, and I have always been drawn to visual arts. It's probably the one thing that's been constant in my life, and I try to include it in everything I do. I started with traditional mediums first (pencil and paper) and then transitioned into digital illustration and graphic design.

I like to make everything look nice and presentable — be it my clothes (I also love ethical fashion), my writing, and of course — my development work. It's a big reason why I do web development. I find that there's plenty of ways to get creative with it.

When I'm not doing "programming-related" stuff, I'm usually shopping. I also volunteer as a designer for student-run organizations. It's a way for me to contribute doing what I'm good at, and to improve at this hobby.

If you meet me in real life, the first thing you'd probably notice is that I'm quite reserved. I am small in appearance, and I don't talk much. I don't think I'm scary though (in contrast — I'm usually intimated by other people.) So if you see me in real life, you can say hi!

Anyways, that's a brief introduction of me.

What I'll be writing about

I do like writing enough to enjoy essay-based university courses as my electives. What I hope to write about in the "personal" section are ramblings about different struggles, happy moments, or interesting things that I come across... This section will be different from the previous two. The notes you'll find in the "computer science & dev" and "design & art" sections are instructional — it's objective and technical in tone.

On the other hand, the "personal" section will be more subjective and opinionated. What can you expect? I've made a list so that it's easier to digest:


  • My experiences as a woman in computer science — there are both negative and positive aspects about it. It's an interesting topic to write about because it's kind of (?) controversial. I'll be writing about my experience in academia, at work, and interactions with the non-tech community.
  • Balancing non-tech hobbies with a tech career — as I've said before, visual arts has always been my favourite thing to do. I'll write about my struggle in nurturing this hobby with everything that a computer science career demands. I found that there are expectations for those wanting career in computer science, such as coding during one's free time, side projects, etc. I'll be writing about how these expectations affect those who, like me, enjoy other non-tech hobbies.
  • Relationship things (romantic and non-romantic) — veering into the non-tech related topics. This one is self-explanatory. I'll be writing about dating scenes, romantic relationships, family relationships, all their expectations and dynamics, and everything in between.
  • Exploring ethics in consumerism — this one is becoming a personal passion. I am someone who loves the thrill of shiny and new things, and I know I need to slow down. Ethics in consumerism covers a pretty broad array of topics. There are issues relating to environmental sustainability, fair treatment of workers, and consumers.

Of course, I may write about other topics. And I may not write about the topics above... but these are just things that I have quite a lot to say about.
